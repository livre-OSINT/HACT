The 13 HACT tactics

HACT organises the adversary's playbook into thirteen tactics (TA-H001 to TA-H013), each mapped to its MITRE ATT&CK® equivalent. Together they describe the full arc of a social-engineering operation, from reconnaissance to impact.

TA-H001 — Target Intelligence↔ MITRE ATT&CK TA0043 Reconnaissance

Collect enough information to identify the target's exploitable vulnerabilities before any contact.

TA-H002 — Operational Preparation↔ TA0042 Resource Development

Prepare resources before contact — cover identities, pretexts, infrastructure. The least detectable phase, conducted entirely at a distance.

TA-H003 — Initial Contact↔ TA0001 Initial Access

Establish the first contact vector; as in cybersecurity, most operations succeed or fail at this phase.

TA-H004 — Legend & Cover Maintenance↔ TA0005 Defense Evasion

Maintain cover credibility throughout, detecting and neutralizing the target's verification attempts.

TA-H005 — Rapport Engineering↔ TA0003 Persistence

Anchor a durable relationship of trust — preserving influence over, or proximity to, the target over time.

TA-H006 — Psychological Exploitation↔ TA0002 Execution

Activate the human vulnerabilities identified during reconnaissance. This is the core of the attack.

TA-H007 — Access Escalation↔ TA0004 Privilege Escalation

Cross a psychological threshold that opens access to more sensitive information.

TA-H008 — Network Discovery↔ TA0007 Discovery

Map the target's informational environment — human links, tools and habits.

TA-H009 — Social Network Traversal↔ TA0008 Lateral Movement

Extend beyond the initial target by exploiting their connections; one entry point can compromise a whole network.

TA-H010 — Intelligence Collection↔ TA0009 Collection

Extract the target information — personal, professional, emotional or financial.

TA-H011 — Handler Communication↔ TA0011 Command & Control

Maintain the psychological link and relational dominance over the target.

TA-H012 — Intelligence Exfiltration↔ TA0010 Exfiltration

Transfer the collected intelligence — verbal, emotional or behavioral information.

TA-H013 — Influence & Impact↔ TA0040 Impact

Produce a real, measurable operational effect on the target — the strategic culmination of the operation.

HACT vs MITRE ATT&CK®

ATT&CK describes technical actions against systems; HACT describes cognitive maneuvers against decisions. Same two-level structure (tactics → techniques), different object.

Architecture
CVE → HVE mapping
Persuasion principles

HVE (Human Vulnerabilities and Exploits) are the human equivalent of CVEs: not software bugs but exploitable cognitive biases and psychological needs. A CVE is patched; an HVE is compensated for with structured vigilance.
